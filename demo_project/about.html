About Amazon

We're guided by four principles: customer obsession rather than competitor focus, passion for invention, commitment to operational excellence, and long-term thinking.

Our Mission

Amazon strives to be Earth's most customer-centric company, Earth's best employer, and Earth's safest place to work. We are driven by the excitement of building technologies, inventing products, and providing services that change lives.

We embrace new ways of doing things, make decisions quickly, and are not afraid to fail. We have the scope and capabilities of a large company, and the spirit and heart of a small one.

Our Core Values

🎯

Customer Obsession

Leaders start with the customer and work backwards. They work vigorously to earn and keep customer trust.

💡

Invent & Simplify

Leaders expect and require innovation and invention from their teams and always find ways to simplify.

🚀

Bias for Action

Speed matters in business. Many decisions and actions are reversible and do not need extensive study.

🌍

Think Big

Thinking small is a self-fulfilling prophecy. Leaders create and communicate a bold direction that inspires results.

🤝

Earn Trust

Leaders listen attentively, speak candidly, and treat others respectfully. They are vocally self-critical.

🏆

Deliver Results

Leaders focus on the key inputs for their business and deliver them with the right quality and in a timely fashion.

Our Journey

1994

The Beginning

Jeff Bezos founded Amazon in his garage in Bellevue, Washington, starting as an online bookstore.

1997

Going Public

Amazon went public, raising $54 million, and quickly expanded beyond books to music, DVDs, and more.

2005

Amazon Prime

Launched Amazon Prime, offering free two-day shipping and laying the groundwork for a massive membership program.

2006

AWS Launches

Amazon Web Services launched, pioneering cloud computing and becoming a major revenue driver.

2014

Alexa & Echo

Introduced Amazon Echo and the Alexa voice assistant, revolutionizing smart home technology.

2020+

Global Leader

Amazon became one of the most valuable companies in the world, serving hundreds of millions of customers globally.
